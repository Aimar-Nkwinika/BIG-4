    Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)

    Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) are critical for consistency, efficiency, and risk management within the IT department. Below are the primary SOPs needed for our network infrastructure project at uThukela.

    Reasons for SOPs

    Consistency, Efficiency, Training, Compliance, Quality Control, Accountability, Continuity, Risk Management, Communication, and Continuous Improvement are key reasons for implementing SOPs.

    Required SOPs

    • IT Department Staff Structure: Defines roles, responsibilities, and reporting structure within the IT team, including positions like IT Director, Network Manager, and System Administrator.
    • IT Security Policy: Includes user access control, network and data security, incident response, and regular audits to safeguard the network and data integrity.
    • Acceptable Use Policy: Outlines guidelines for responsible use of IT resources, protecting user accounts, respecting network rules, and ensuring data privacy.
    • Acquisition Procedures/Approvals: Details the process for requesting, approving, and acquiring IT equipment or software, ensuring alignment with budget and standards.
    • Service Desk/Help Desk Procedures: Standardizes incident reporting, ticket assignment, resolution processes, and follow-up for efficient issue management.
    • Disaster Plan: Covers critical steps and responsibilities in case of a disaster, including preventive and corrective measures, system recovery, and continuity procedures.

    Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)

    The Disaster Recovery Plan ensures IT systems continuity at uThukela during emergencies, defining roles and processes to mitigate disruptions.

    Key Components:

    • Disaster Notification & Plan Activation: Clear procedures for declaring a disaster, damage assessment, and activating the recovery site.
    • System Recovery Procedures: Steps for reinstalling and testing systems, configuring networks, and re-establishing communication links.
    • Primary Site Procedures: Securing the primary site, assessing damage, and arranging necessary repairs or replacements.
    • Re-establishment of Normal Operations: Restoring systems, testing, and backup to resume regular operations at the primary site.
    • Preventive and Detective Measures: Data backups, redundancy, firewalls, intrusion detection, and other controls to protect the network.
    • Corrective Measures: Data restoration, hardware replacement, network isolation, and security patches post-incident.
    • Maintenance and Testing: Regular testing and updates to the DRP, including training for relevant staff to ensure readiness.
